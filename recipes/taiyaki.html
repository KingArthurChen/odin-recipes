taiyaki

Description

Green tea mochi is a delicious rice cake that comes from Japan. You'll definitely want to try this matcha rice cake if you have an incurable sweet tooth. It's a pretty wagashi, or traditional Japanese sweets, that's a nice light tea-time snack!

Ingredients

  • 170 grams butter (6 ounces, 12 tablespoons)
  • 65 grams light brown sugar (2.3 ounces, 8 tablespoons)
  • 100 grams egg (2 eggs, 3.5 ounces)
  • 240 grams cake flour (8.5 ounces, 1 1/2 cup)
  • 6 grams baking powder (0.2 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 155 grams warm milk (5.5 ounces, 2/3 cup)
  • 10 grams honey (0.35 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 6 grams light soy sauce (0.2 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 4 grams vanilla extract (0.14 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 3 parts sugar / 150 grams (5.3 ounces)
  • 5 parts red beans / 250 grams (8.8 ounces)
  • 10 parts water / 500 grams (17.6 ounces)

Instructions

  1. Pressure cook the adzuki beans with the water for 1 hour.
  2. Pur√©e the beans in a food processor until you achieve a fine, sandy texture. It will seem very dry, but it will be rehydrated in the next step.
  3. Return the beans to a saucepan and add just enough water until the bean paste barely starts to clump together.
  4. Cook the beans with the sugar, stirring until the paste comes together and the sugar fully dissolves into the paste, about 3 minutes.
  5. Pass the beans through a sieve to remove any chunks for a finer paste.
  6. Shape the paste into inserts that will fit inside the fish-shaped taiyaki.
  7. Cream the butter together with the sugar
  8. Beat in one egg, then the other
  9. Beat in half the flour, then the milk, then the other half of flour
  10. Use a squeeze bottle to squeeze a pool of batter into the body, then the tail, and around the body again.
  11. Place the red bean insert into the center of the taiyaki and press in halfway.
  12. Pour more batter over the red bean insert so that it's fully covered.
  13. Check the doneness of each side and if not done, continue grilling at 45-second intervals.
  14. The taiyaki are done when they are evenly golden brown. Remove with a pair of chopsticks.
  15. Serve while still warm and crunchy on the outside. Oishi!
